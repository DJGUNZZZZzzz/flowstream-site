DMCA POLICY

Last Updated: December 16, 2025

COPYRIGHT PROTECTION: FlowStream respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA).

1. Our Commitment

FlowStream is committed to protecting the intellectual property rights of content creators and copyright holders. We take copyright infringement seriously and will respond promptly to valid DMCA notices.

2. Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe that your copyrighted work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement on FlowStream, please provide our Copyright Agent with the following information:

2.1 Required Information

Your DMCA notice must include:

  • Identification of the copyrighted work: A description of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed, or if multiple works, a representative list
  • Identification of the infringing material: A description of where the material you claim is infringing is located on FlowStream (provide URLs or specific locations)
  • Contact information: Your address, telephone number, and email address
  • Good faith statement: A statement that you have a good faith belief that the disputed use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  • Accuracy statement: A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf
  • Physical or electronic signature: Your physical or electronic signature

2.2 Where to Send Notices

FlowStream Copyright Agent
Flow Networks Inc.
DMCA Department
Email: dmca@flowstream.io
Subject Line: "DMCA Takedown Notice"

WARNING: Filing a false or fraudulent DMCA notice may result in legal liability. You may be subject to damages, including costs and attorneys' fees, if you materially misrepresent that content is infringing.

3. Our Response Process

3.1 Review

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:

  • Review the notice for completeness and validity
  • Verify the identity of the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • Assess the alleged infringement

3.2 Action

If the notice is valid, we will:

  • Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
  • Notify the user who posted the material
  • Document the takedown in our records
  • Take appropriate action against repeat infringers

3.3 Timeline

We aim to respond to valid DMCA notices within 24-48 hours. Complex cases may take longer to investigate.

4. Counter-Notification

If you believe that material you posted was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notification.

4.1 Counter-Notification Requirements

Your counter-notification must include:

  • Identification of the material: A description of the material that was removed and its location before removal
  • Good faith statement: A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification
  • Consent to jurisdiction: A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or Delaware if outside the U.S.), and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original DMCA notice
  • Contact information: Your name, address, telephone number, and email address
  • Physical or electronic signature: Your physical or electronic signature

4.2 Where to Send Counter-Notifications

FlowStream Copyright Agent
Flow Networks Inc.
DMCA Department
Email: dmca@flowstream.io
Subject Line: "DMCA Counter-Notification"

4.3 Counter-Notification Process

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification:

  • We will forward it to the original complainant
  • We will inform them that we may restore the removed material in 10-14 business days
  • If the complainant does not file a court action seeking an injunction, we will restore the material

5. Repeat Infringer Policy

5.1 Three-Strike System

FlowStream operates a three-strike policy for copyright infringement:

  • First Strike: Warning and removal of infringing content
  • Second Strike: Temporary suspension (7-30 days) and removal of content
  • Third Strike: Permanent account termination

5.2 Immediate Termination

We reserve the right to immediately terminate accounts that:

  • Engage in egregious or willful infringement
  • Repeatedly ignore DMCA notices
  • Attempt to circumvent our copyright protection measures

6. Content ID System

FlowStream uses automated content identification technology to detect copyrighted material. This system:

  • Scans uploaded content against a database of copyrighted works
  • Automatically flags potential matches
  • Allows copyright owners to choose actions (block, monetize, or track)
  • Provides analytics to copyright owners

7. Safe Harbor

FlowStream qualifies for safe harbor protection under the DMCA as a service provider. We:

  • Do not have actual knowledge of infringing activity
  • Do not receive financial benefit directly attributable to infringing activity
  • Act expeditiously to remove or disable access upon receiving proper notice
  • Have designated a Copyright Agent to receive notifications
  • Implement a repeat infringer policy

8. Fair Use

FlowStream recognizes that some uses of copyrighted material may qualify as fair use under U.S. copyright law. Fair use factors include:

  • Purpose and character of the use (commercial vs. educational)
  • Nature of the copyrighted work
  • Amount and substantiality of the portion used
  • Effect on the potential market for the work

Fair use is determined on a case-by-case basis. If you believe your use qualifies as fair use, you may file a counter-notification.

9. International Copyright

FlowStream respects international copyright laws and treaties, including:

  • Berne Convention
  • WIPO Copyright Treaty
  • EU Copyright Directive

We will honor valid copyright notices from any jurisdiction.

10. Preventing Infringement

10.1 User Responsibilities

To avoid copyright infringement:

  • Only upload content you own or have permission to use
  • Obtain proper licenses for music, images, and video clips
  • Give credit to original creators
  • Understand fair use limitations
  • Remove content if you receive a DMCA notice

10.2 Resources

For more information about copyright:

  • U.S. Copyright Office
  • Fair Use Guidelines
  • DMCA.com Resources
