COOKIE POLICY

Last Updated: December 16, 2025

ABOUT COOKIES: This Cookie Policy explains how FlowStream uses cookies and similar tracking technologies.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, making your next visit easier and the site more useful to you.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Platform to function and cannot be disabled:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
session_id Maintains your login session Session
auth_token Authenticates your account 30 days
csrf_token Security protection against attacks Session
cookie_consent Remembers your cookie preferences 1 year

2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
user_preferences Stores your settings and preferences 1 year
theme_mode Remembers light/dark mode preference 1 year
language Stores your language preference 1 year
volume_level Remembers your volume settings 6 months

2.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use the Platform:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
_ga Google Analytics - distinguishes users 2 years
_gid Google Analytics - distinguishes users 24 hours
_gat Google Analytics - throttles request rate 1 minute
analytics_session Tracks session duration and interactions Session

2.4 Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements:

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
ad_id Identifies you for targeted advertising 90 days
ad_preferences Stores your ad preferences 1 year
conversion_tracking Measures ad campaign effectiveness 30 days

3. Other Tracking Technologies

3.1 Local Storage

We use browser local storage to:

  • Store Netrunner University progress
  • Cache frequently accessed data
  • Remember your watch history
  • Store Flow Token balance

3.2 Web Beacons (Pixels)

Small transparent images that help us:

  • Track email open rates
  • Measure page views
  • Understand user behavior

3.3 Session Replay

We may use session replay tools to:

  • Understand how users navigate the Platform
  • Identify usability issues
  • Improve user experience

Session replays are anonymized and do not capture sensitive information like passwords or payment details.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use third-party services that may set their own cookies:

4.1 Google Analytics

Provides website analytics and insights. Google Privacy Policy

4.2 Payment Processors

Stripe and PayPal may set cookies during checkout. See their privacy policies for details.

4.3 Social Media

Social media sharing buttons may set cookies from platforms like Twitter, Discord, and YouTube.

4.4 Advertising Networks

Third-party ad networks may use cookies to deliver targeted ads.

5. How to Control Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (may affect functionality)
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser

Browser-specific instructions:

  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Safari
  • Edge

5.2 Our Cookie Consent Tool

When you first visit FlowStream, you can choose which types of cookies to accept. You can change your preferences at any time in your account settings.

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain tracking:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out
  • NAI Opt-out
  • DAA Opt-out

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, some features may not work properly:

  • You may need to log in repeatedly
  • Your preferences won't be saved
  • Some features may be unavailable
  • The Platform may not function correctly

7. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.
